THE ST. PAUL
SANDWICH

If you grew up in St. Louis, you know exactly what this is. The St. Paul Sandwich was born in the Chinese restaurants of North St. Louis — a crispy egg foo young patty pressed between soft white bread, stacked with mayo, pickles, lettuce, and tomato. You got it from the window on Lindbergh. You ordered it by the bag. Lou Wok brought it to Phoenix, made exactly the way you remember it.

Home Base
North STL to Phoenix

Lou Wok Rice House is rooted in the Chinese restaurant culture of St. Louis, Missouri — the rice houses of North County, the corner spots on Lindbergh, the places where your family ordered by the bucket and the St. Paul sandwich came wrapped in wax paper. That's the food we grew up on. That's what we brought to Phoenix.

Lou Wok is for the transplants who couldn't find it here. For the St. Louis expat who still dreams about the rice house on the corner. For anyone who wants to know what real wok-fired STL-style fried rice tastes like. Every plate is made to order. The way it was always supposed to be.

The STL Rice House Tradition

St. Louis has a unique Chinese-American food culture unlike anywhere else in the country — egg foo young, wok fried rice by the half and whole, crab rangoon with every order, and the St. Paul sandwich that no other city has. Lou Wok carries that tradition west.
