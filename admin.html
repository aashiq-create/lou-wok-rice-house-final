📦 Inventory

Track how much of each ingredient and supply you have on hand, in bulk. Set a low-stock threshold per item — when the count hits it or below, you'll get an alert on the Dashboard. Remember to Publish after saving.

Ingredients & Supplies

Track everything you buy in bulk — rice, oil, soy sauce, eggs, bread, produce, plus plasticware, napkins, boxes, etc. Tap “+ Add item” to add one, or “Scan barcode” to attach a product barcode so you can restock it by scanning next time. Use the × to delete anything you no longer track.

📷 How does scanning work? (tap to read)

First time you scan an item: point your phone camera at the barcode. It creates a new supply row with the barcode attached — just type in the name (e.g. “8oz plastic cups”) and set your quantity.

Every time after: scan that same barcode and it instantly finds the item and adds +1 to the count — fast for restocking a delivery.

On iPhone: the camera scans QR codes directly. For regular product barcodes (the striped UPC kind), iPhone can't scan them in the browser — use the “enter by hand” box that appears and type the number under the barcode. On Android it scans both automatically.

Tip: the barcode is just a number that identifies the product — you always name the item yourself. Allow camera access when your phone asks. Remember to Save and Publish when done.

📅 Upcoming Events & Locations

Date-specific events (festivals, markets, brewery slots) shown in the site's Find the Truck section as upcoming locations. Import a calendar file (.ics or .csv), or add events by hand. Remember to Publish after saving.

📎 CSV format & import help (tap)

.ics files from Google/Apple/Outlook calendars import directly — every event with a date, title, and location is picked up.

.csv files should have a header row with these columns (any order): date, time, title, location, note. Example row:
2026-11-07,17:00,Canal Convergence,Scottsdale Waterfront,Apply to vend

Imported events are added to your list (duplicates by date+title are skipped), so you can import multiple files.

Controls the cart/checkout on the live site. Until your Square account and a payment endpoint are connected, checkout collects the customer's details and places the order as “pay at pickup.” Add the Square IDs below when ready to enable card payment.
